# 📊 Customer Segmentation: Retail Sales & Profitability Insights

## 📌 Overview
This project analyzes retail transaction data to understand customer behavior, segment performance, and profitability patterns across regions and product categories. The goal is to provide data-driven insights that support strategic decisions in sales, marketing, and operations.

## 🎯 Objectives
- Analyze customer distribution across segments and regions
- Identify high-performing and underperforming product categories
- Understand the impact of discounting on profitability
- Provide actionable business recommendations through interactive dashboards

## 📂 Dataset
The dataset contains US retail transaction records from 2014 to 2017, including:
- Customer ID, Name & Segment
- Order & Shipping details
- Product Category & Sub-category
- Sales, Quantity, Discount & Profit
- Region, State & City

> **Source:** Sample Superstore Dataset | **Records:** 9,994 rows × 21 columns

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies
- Power BI Desktop
- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
- Microsoft Excel

## 🔍 Key Analysis Steps

### 1. Data Exploration
- Reviewed dataset structure, column types, and value ranges
- Identified key dimensions: Segment, Region, Category, Ship Mode
- Examined date range and temporal coverage (2014–2017)

### 2. Data Preparation
- Verified data consistency and completeness
- Formatted date columns and numeric fields
- Established relationships between data tables in Power BI

### 3. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Customer distribution by segment (Consumer, Corporate, Home Office)
- Sales and profit trends by region (East, West, Central, South)
- Category-level performance (Furniture, Office Supplies, Technology)
- Shipping mode usage and fulfillment patterns

### 4. Segmentation Analysis
- Compared revenue and profit across customer segments
- Identified top and bottom performing sub-categories
- Analyzed discount rate impact on profit margins
- Measured sales growth trends year over year

## 📊 Key Insights
- The **Consumer** segment generates the highest sales volume but also has the heaviest discount burden
- The **Technology** category delivers the highest profit margins
- Discounts above **40%** consistently result in negative profit margins
- The **West** region leads in total revenue; the **South** is the lowest performer
- **Furniture** has the lowest profitability despite decent sales volume

## 📈 Business Recommendations
- Revisit discount strategy — cap discounts to protect profit margins
- Focus upselling efforts on Technology products in high-performing regions
- Investigate underperformance in the South region (pricing, logistics, demand)
- Develop loyalty programs targeting the Consumer segment for retention
- Review Furniture category pricing to improve its contribution margin
